VERSE 1 – JUST & ALREADY
I have just done it. I have just seen it.
I have just said it. Put it in the middle, yeah, that's it.
I have already done it. I have already seen it.
I have already said it. Put it in the middle, yeah, that's it.
VERSE 2 – YET
I haven't done it yet. I haven't seen it yet.
I haven't said it yet. Put it at the end, keep the flow on track.
Have you done it yet? Have you seen it yet?
Have you said it yet? Put it at the end, don't look back.
CHORUS – FOR & SINCE (rule)
How long have you been here? How long, how long...
VERSE 3 – FOR & SINCE
I've been here for ten minutes. I've been here for two hours.
I've been here for five years. FOR is the duration, show your powers.
I've been here since Monday. I've been here since breakfast.
I've been here since ten o'clock. SINCE is the start, I'm the fastest.
📌 Quick Rules
JUST → very recently → I have just eaten.
ALREADY → sooner than expected → I have already finished.
YET → in negatives & questions, at the END → I haven't finished yet. / Have you finished yet?
FOR → duration → I've been here for two hours.
SINCE → starting point → I've been here since Monday.

🗣️ Free Practice
Attività orali e scritte per usare tutte e 5 le strutture.
Activity 1 – Just & Already (pair work)3 min
Student A
Ask: Have you done your homework yet? / Have you eaten yet? / Have you read the book yet?

Student B
Answer using just or already:
"Yes, I've just done it!" (very recently) OR "Yes, I've already done it!" (before expected)
Activity 2 – Yet (negative)3 min
Student A
Ask 5 questions with yet: Have you (watched the new series / visited Rome / learned to drive / cooked pasta) yet?

Student B
Answer honestly: No, I haven't ___ yet. OR Yes, I've already ___!
Activity 3 – For & Since 🕐4 min
Student A
Ask: How long have you studied English? / How long have you lived in [city]? / How long have you had your phone?

Student B
Answer with for (duration) or since (start point):
"I've studied English for 5 years."
"I've lived here since 2018."
Activity 4 – Mix it all! 🎯5 min
Solo / Pair
Write 5 true sentences about yourself using all 5 adverbs:

1. I've just ___
2. I've already ___
3. I haven't ___ yet.
4. I've known my best friend for ___
5. I've had my phone since ___
