📋 Quick Rules — DO vs DOES ▼ show
I / YOU / WE / THEY DO you drink milk? — Yes, I DO. / No, I DON'T.
HE / SHE / IT DOES she drink milk? — Yes, she DOES. / No, she DOESN'T.
⚠️ With HE/SHE/IT → use DOES/DOESN'T · the main verb has NO -s in questions/negatives

Free Practice

Activity 1 — Speaking: Yes or No? 👥 Pairs
Ask your partner about their food habits using Do you…? They answer with Yes, I do or No, I don't. Then swap roles.
Do you eat fish?
→ Yes, I do! / No, I don't.
Try at least 5 questions each: milk · fish · bread · rice · fries · fruit · juice · cake · steak · eggs
Activity 2 — Speaking: Ask about them 👥 Pairs
Think of a friend or family member. Your partner asks you questions with Does he/she…? You answer for that person.
Does your brother eat fries?
→ Yes, he does! / No, he doesn't.
Activity 3 — Writing: 5 sentences ✏️ Individual
Write 5 sentences about someone you know (friend, brother, sister, parent…). Use does/doesn't. Mix positive and negative.
My sister likes eggs. She doesn't drink milk.
My brother eats fish. He doesn't want cake.
⚠️ Remember: He eats fish. (positive) · He doesn't eat fish. (no -s after doesn't!)
